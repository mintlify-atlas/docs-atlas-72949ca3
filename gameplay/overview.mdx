fheroes2 faithfully recreates the Heroes of Might and Magic II experience: explore a fantasy map, gather resources, build up castles, recruit armies, and defeat your enemies through a mix of strategic planning and tactical combat. Every game mode — solo campaign, standalone scenario, or hot-seat multiplayer — follows the same turn-based structure, letting you play at your own pace while the AI takes its turn.

## Core gameplay

Heroes of Might and Magic II is a turn-based strategy game. On each turn you move heroes across the adventure map, visit resource mines and special locations, recruit troops, build castle structures, and end your turn when you are ready. Opponents — human or AI — take their turns in sequence. Victory conditions vary by scenario but typically involve defeating all enemy heroes and castles, or reaching a specific objective.

### Resources and economy

Seven resource types drive your economy: Gold, Wood, Ore, Sulfur, Crystal, Mercury, and Gems. You collect them by capturing mines scattered across the map or by building the appropriate structures in your castles. Gold is needed for almost everything; rarer resources are required for higher-tier buildings and creatures.

### Castles and buildings

Each castle belongs to one of six town types (Knight, Barbarian, Sorceress, Warlock, Wizard, Necromancer), each with a unique set of buildings and creatures. Constructing buildings unlocks stronger troop tiers, mage guilds for spells, marketplaces, and defensive structures like moats and towers. One building can be constructed per day.

### Heroes

Heroes lead your armies. They gain experience from battles, learn skills, and collect artifacts that boost their combat effectiveness. Heroes can also carry spells from a mage guild. Losing all your heroes and towns ends the game for your side.

## Game modes

| Mode | Description |
|---|---|
| Standard campaign | Follows the storyline of Roland or Archibald across linked scenarios with carried-over heroes and bonuses. Difficulty can be selected before starting. |
| Standalone scenario | A single map played to completion. Choose from the bundled scenarios or load a community map. |
| Hot-seat multiplayer | Multiple players take turns on the same device. Up to six players are supported. |
| Battle only | Arrange two armies and resolve a single combat — useful for testing unit matchups. |

## Battle system

When two opposing forces meet on the adventure map, combat takes place on a hex grid. Armies act in initiative order, displayed in the turn order panel. On your turn, select a unit to move it, attack an adjacent enemy, or cast a spell from your hero's spell book.

Key battle mechanics:

- **Movement**: Each unit type has a speed and a movement range. The reachable hexes are highlighted when you select a unit.
- **Attack**: Melee units must move adjacent to an enemy. Ranged units (archers, mages) can shoot across the battlefield with no movement required until enemy units approach.
- **Spells**: Your hero can cast one spell per round from the spell book, spending spell points. Spell points replenish when visiting a mage guild or resting.
- **Morale and luck**: High morale gives a unit a chance to act twice in a row; high luck can double damage. Low values produce negative effects.
- **Retreat and surrender**: You can retreat (hero flees, losing the army) or surrender (pay gold to preserve the hero).

## Hero and castle management

You can assign up to five different creature stacks to a hero's army. Use **Ctrl**, **Alt**, and **Shift** while dragging to rearrange army slots quickly. Splitting stacks lets you spread units across slots for greater tactical flexibility. The castle well lets you recruit all available creatures in one step rather than visiting each dwelling individually.

Heroes can visit shrines and witch huts on the adventure map to learn spells and secondary skills. After visiting, the location shows which spell or skill it contains so you can plan future hero routes.

## Save system

fheroes2 maintains its own save file format. Save files include the map name, game progress in days, and the file location within your operating system. You can delete saves and scroll through long save lists directly from the load dialog.

<Warning>
  fheroes2 save files are **not compatible** with save files from the original Heroes of Might and Magic II. If you have saves from the original game, they cannot be loaded in fheroes2.
</Warning>
